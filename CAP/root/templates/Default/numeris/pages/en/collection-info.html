Introduction

Background

Since 1944 Numeris* has collected radio and television audience data through several methodologies in numerous markets across Canada, providing its members with invaluable insights into Canadians’ viewing and listening habits. Whether they’re broadcasters, agencies, advertisers or other interested organizations, they need to know who is viewing or listening to what, and how many people are viewing or listening.

Reports with audience data for the measured markets, compiled into a set of books for each survey conducted between 1944 and mid-2009, were originally available strictly to Numeris members. This historical material is now accessible to the public through these archives. Numeris plans to include material from mid-2009 in the future.

Diary Survey Books

From 1944 to mid-2009, audience data collected through surveys was processed and the results printed in reports included in a set of books (e.g. the Fall 1982 television books), from which particular books were then distributed to Numeris’s members according to their markets of interest. A typical set of survey books, either for radio or television, includes reports on every surveyed market, with some markets reported in individual Market books (e.g. a “Toronto” television book), or multiple markets compiled in Regional books (e.g. an “Atlantic” radio book containing Halifax, St. John’s, Moncton, among other markets). A set of books also includes Reach reports, Special reports requested by members, and Reference Material pertaining to the survey.

Survey Frequency, Markets Measured and Book Format Variations

The number of surveys conducted in a year has varied over time, starting with one radio survey in 1944 every two years, with frequency increasing to twice a year in 1956, which also included the introduction of the television service. Survey frequency increased to four times a year in 1963, with a variation in frequency thereafter. The Fall survey has typically been the major survey with all measured markets included, followed by the Spring survey. Winter and Summer surveys, when present, have typically included only major markets, as well as special surveys for some of those markets. Other special surveys may have been conducted in a year at the request of particular members, with results compiled and provided to those members.

Markets measured from one year to another for the same type of survey (e.g. Fall survey) may also vary, although not considerably.

The number of books printed for a survey, as well as the distribution of markets among books (i.e. markets included in individual Market books and markets in Regional books), varied over time due to different factors. For example, changes to the report content and/or format, such as the addition of demographic groups or type of audience estimates to the reports, resulted in larger reports that had to be split in separate books (e.g. a Regional book split into Market books and/or smaller Regional books). The number of books (and markets included in a book) among surveys within a year also varied, depending on the survey; a Fall survey typically includes all measured markets, while a Summer survey only major markets.

For a graphical summary of the evolution of the radio and television Market and Regional books, see figures Radio Market and Regional Reports Through the Years and Television Market and Regional Reports through the Years below.

Diary Surveys

One of the methodologies Numeris has employed consists of collecting radio listening or TV viewing data through diaries. Diaries are essentially weekly surveys that capture the radio listening or TV viewing habits of individuals living in randomly selected households across various Canadian markets.

Diary surveys are identified in the collection by the year and the closest season’s name in which they were conducted (e.g. Fall 1970). Additional surveys in a year, either conducted for major markets only, or at the request of a particular member, are identified in the collection by the closest season’s name and year, and a prefix such as “pre” or “post” (meaning before or after the main survey, not necessarily the season dates in a calendar year), or a similar identifier (e.g. “special”), to differentiate them from the main surveys (e.g. “Pre Fall 1975”).

Radio Collection

The Radio collection contains material related to the radio measurement. The Radio collection is comprised primarily of Market Reports and Regional Reports (also referred to as Market or Regional books). The collection also contains Reach Reports and Special Reports, outlined below.

Note: Radio stations are identified in the collection by the call letter used by the C.R.T.C. An AM radio station’s call letters is 4-character long (e.g. CAAA); a few 3-character exceptions exist. A FM radio station’s call letter has a 4-character base (a few 3-character exceptions may exist), followed by either “FM” or “-FM” depending on the type of report, report section or year. These variations should be considered when searching call letters in the collection.

Radio Market and Regional Reports

The Radio Market and Regional Reports contain audience estimates on radio listening for each member station in a measured market. Information on a market may be included in a Market Report (e.g. a Toronto Market Report), or together with other markets within the same region in a Regional Report (e.g. the Halifax market in the Atlantic Regional Report). For a graphical summary of the evolution of the Radio Market and Regional Reports, see figure Radio Market and Regional Reports Through the Years below.

Market and Regional Reports feature the content below. For clarity, a Market or a Regional Report is a collection of reports related to the market(s).

  • Current Survey Information – The following information may be included depending on the year:

    • Important notifications (e.g. survey disruptions, violations to the rules and regulations by a station)
    • Report content description
    • Survey notes
    • Central area map
    • Tabulated sample and population
    • Station facilities and format / format changes
    • Major league sporting events by station
    • Off-air and reduced power periods by station
    • Time block index by station (if applicable)
    • Listening levels

  • Reports

    • Section 1 – Station Reports
      • Station Report (a report per station): Provides audience estimates for a station within a number of time blocks (e.g. 7:00 a.m. 7:00 p.m. Saturday) and quarter-hour by quarter-hour.
      • Supplementary Reports (if any): Other than being based on a redefined geographical area and/or addition of station combinations requested by a commissioning member station, the report is identical to a regular station report.
    • Section 2 – Group Reports
      • Group Reports (a report per station group): Provides combined audience estimates of two or more stations that sell advertising time at package rates.
      • Supplementary Group Reports (if any).
    • Section 3 – Market Reports –The following information may be included depending on the year.
      • Market Reports (a report per market): Provides audience estimates of stations by area.
      • Population Profile by Market (added in 1986): Provides a profile of a Central Market's population, with qualitative data on age, sex, occupation, education, language, household size, for a number of demographic groups, which can be compared with the profile of an individual station in that Central Market.
      • Market Trends (added in 1986): Enables a comparison of the performance of two or more stations in the same area, or current versus past surveys.

  • Additional Information – The following information may be included depending on the year, either as introduction or as appendix:

    • Description of occupation categories
    • Description of format types
    • Glossary of selected radio terms
    • Survey techniques
    • Additional services
    • Rules and regulations

Radio Reach Reports

Provides reach, total hours (%/(000)) and average hours for several demographics presented as the top member stations in each reported market and area as well as the primary areas covered by each station.

  • Current Survey Information – The following information may be included depending on the year:

    • Important notifications (e.g. survey disruptions, violations to the rules and regulations by a station)
    • Area maps
    • Tabulated sample and population
    • Station index
    • Provincial listening levels

  • Reports

    • Reach by Station: Stations listed by province (from east to west), alphabetical by area and call letter, areas listed in descending order according to total hours of listening in that area.
    • Reach by Area: Within a reporting area (listed from east to west), stations are listed in descending order according to total of hours of listening in that area.

Television Collection

The Television collection contains material related to the television measurement. The Television collection is comprised primarily of Market Reports and early Regional Reports (also referred to as Market or Regional books). The collection also contains Reach Reports and Special Reports, outlined below.

Note: In diary-related documents, a local television station (conventional) is typically identified by the 4-character call letter used by the C.R.T.C. Specialty stations may contain a maximum of 6 characters. This size limitation must be considered when searching the collection, as a station’s full name may not be found in the content.

Television Market and Regional Reports

The Television Market and Regional Reports contain audience estimates on television viewing for each station in a measured market. Information on a market may be included in a Market Report (e.g. a Toronto Market Report), or together with other markets within the same region in a Regional Report (e.g. the Halifax market in the Atlantic Regional Report). For a graphical summary of the evolution of the Television Market and Regional Reports, see figure Television Market and Regional Reports Through the Years below.

Market and Regional Reports feature the content below. For clarity, a Market or a Regional Report is a collection of reports related to the market(s).

  • Current Survey Information – The following information may be included depending on the year:

    • Notices to members
    • Report content description
    • Sample size and population for the market
    • A map showing the counties or census divisions comprising the market. As well, the boundaries of both the central and extended markets are identified.
    • A list of television stations that are reported and their network affiliation.
    • A movie index of films carried by each station during the survey period – as of Fall 1980

  • Report – (Early reports may contain less detail)

    • Time Block Section: Provides audience estimates for stations for specific periods of time during the day (e.g. Monday to Sunday, from 7:00 p.m. to 11:00 p.m.)
    • Program Section: Provides an alphabetical listing of programs carried by stations during the survey period, with audience estimates. Earlier reports (1975 and earlier) do not have this section; programs are listed by time period.
    • Time Period Section: Provides audience estimates for stations by equal periods (e.g. half-hour by half-hour).
    • “F.C. inc. cancom” Section: For markets with stations carried on the satellite coast-to-coast, showing total audiences by station across Canada – from 1983 to 2001
    • Market trends (added in 1992): Enables a comparison of the performance of two or more stations in the same area, or current versus past surveys.

  • Additional Information – The following information may be included depending on the year, either as introduction or as appendix:

    • Survey method/techniques
    • Glossary of selected television terms
    • Rules and regulations

Television Reach Reports

  • Current Survey Information – The following information may be included depending on the year:

  • Reports

    • Reach by Area: Within a reporting area (listed from east to west), stations are listed in descending order according to total of hours of viewing in that area.
